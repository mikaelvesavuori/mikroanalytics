title: Comparison
description: How MikroAnalytics compares with other analytics tools.
---

MikroAnalytics is for teams that want useful web and product signals without visitor profiling or a managed analytics account. It favors ownership, clear dashboards, and a small deployment surface over large marketing suites.

It is not meant to be better than every alternative. It is meant to be a better fit when ownership, simplicity, and a smaller operational surface matter.

## Quick Comparison

| Area | MikroAnalytics | Common alternatives |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hosting | Self-hosted app and API | Managed SaaS, self-hosted, or both |
| Data model | Privacy-oriented events and dashboards | Ranges from simple page views to full behavioral platforms |
| Privacy | Designed around no visitor profiles | Depends heavily on product and configuration |
| Operations | Small service you own | Managed convenience or larger self-hosted stacks |
| Best fit | Owned analytics for small products and sites | Ad attribution, warehouse-scale analytics, or enterprise BI |

## Where MikroAnalytics Fits

- You want analytics without building visitor profiles.
- You prefer self-hosting and predictable ownership.
- You need enough signal to improve a product, not a full marketing cloud.

## When Another Tool May Be Better

- You need ad network attribution, cohort tooling, or data warehouse workflows.
- You need managed compliance, SLAs, or large-scale analytics operations.
- You want a broad product analytics suite with experimentation and session replay.

## Bottom Line

Choose MikroAnalytics when you want a focused tool you can understand, run, and keep. Choose a larger platform when you need its scale, ecosystem, managed operations, or specialized workflows.
